# Character Biographies

## Working Draft — Needs Development

### The Family (Provisional Names)

#### GIOVANNI ESPOSITO — The Father

- **Age:** 35
- **Origin:** Small village near Naples, Campania
- **Occupation in Italy:** Sharecropper / farm laborer
- **Occupation in New York:** Construction laborer / dock worker
- **Personality:** Proud, stubborn, deeply religious, determined to provide for his family. Speaks Neapolitan dialect. Limited literacy.
- **Internal conflict:** The shame of leaving his homeland vs. the necessity of providing for his children. The struggle to maintain authority in a world that doesn't respect him.
- **Key scenes:** The departure decision; the Ellis Island staircase; his first day of labor in New York.

#### MARIA ESPOSITO — The Mother

- **Age:** 30
- **Origin:** Same village (married Giovanni at 16)
- **Occupation in Italy:** Domestic work, field work during harvest
- **Occupation in New York:** Piecework sewing at home; takes in boarders
- **Personality:** Resilient, practical, deeply attached to tradition and religious practice. The emotional center of the family. Speaks Neapolitan dialect.
- **Internal conflict:** Homesickness that never fades. The terror of losing a child to American disease or accident. The desire to preserve Italian traditions vs. the need to adapt.
- **Key scenes:** The steerage cooking; the Ellis Island waiting room; making the tenement into a home.

#### LUIGI ESPOSITO — The Eldest Son

- **Age:** 12
- **Role:** The bridge between old and new worlds. Learns English fastest. Works as a newsboy or shoeshine boy.
- **Personality:** Adventurous, clever, loyal to family but drawn to American life.
- **Internal conflict:** The pull of the streets vs. loyalty to his parents' world. Growing up too fast.

#### CARMELA ESPOSITO — The Daughter

- **Age:** 8
- **Role:** The innocent — the character who represents hope and the future.
- **Personality:** Quiet, observant, attaches to people easily.
- **Key detail:** She carries a small religious medal from her grandmother — her only personal possession from Italy.

#### ANNA ESPOSITO — The Youngest

- **Age:** 3
- **Role:** The vulnerable child — represents the family's greatest fear and greatest love.
- **Key detail:** Nearly rejected at Ellis Island due to a cough (detained briefly, then cleared).

### Supporting Characters

#### PASQUALE — The Paesano in New York

- **Origin:** Same village as the Espositos; emigrated 2 years earlier
- **Role:** The connection — he arranged for Giovanni's work through a padrone. He meets them at the Battery.
- **Personality:** Already partially Americanized but still loyal to his paesani. Knows the streets, the systems, the dangers.
- **Internal conflict:** His role as intermediary between the family and American systems vs. his own survival needs.

#### THE PADRONE — Don Michele

- **Origin:** Northern Italy (creates a cultural tension)
- **Role:** Labor broker — provides work but takes a significant cut
- **Personality:** Business-minded, not entirely villainous but exploitative. Represents the system that traps immigrants.
- **Notes:** Could be a complex character — not simply evil, but a product of the same immigration system.

#### THE INTERPRETER — Signor Martelli

- **Origin:** Italian-American, second generation
- **Role:** Ellis Island interpreter who helps the family through legal inspection
- **Personality:** Compassionate but overwhelmed by the daily volume. Speaks standard Italian and several dialects.
- **Key scene:** The legal inspection — translating the 29 questions, navigating the family's fear.

#### THE MEDICAL INSPECTOR — Dr. Parker

- **Role:** Ellis Island physician who conducts the six-second medical exam
- **Personality:** Professional, efficient, not cruel but detached by necessity. Has seen thousands of immigrants.
- **Key scene:** The chalk mark moment — a terrifying instant when he pauses at Anna's cough.
